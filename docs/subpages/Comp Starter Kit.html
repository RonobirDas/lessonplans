Computer Architecture

Long awaited

Phase 1 - Intro

Everyone’s gotta start somewhere, why not here? It is a two fold combination to start.

Read:
The essential and standard intro undergrad textbook used everywhere

H&P COD 4th Edition

Why the 4th edition? It’s cheaper and the 5th edition removed the GPU section. What??? WHY?!

Watch:
CMU ECE 447

It’s the greatness himself, Onur Mutlu. Women want him, men want to be him. James Bond of Comp Arch. Awards for being one of the youngest faculty with so many accomplishments, graduate from UTA.

I advise looking through the entire course, not just the videos. He provides a lot of good stuff there.

Phase 2 - Completing Fundamentals

Once you finish this you’ll be at an intermediate level in computer architecture, or first year masters student/adept senior. Far from specialization. This is the turning point, you’ll find out if you like this field at all.

Read:
H&P CA:AQA

read the appendix sections as well. All of them preferrably. That’ll take you awhile since there’s A - J afaik, like 600+ pages, so good luck with that.

Readings in Computer Architecture

An essential complement to AQA.

Modern Processor Design: Fundamentals of Superscalar Processors

Far from modern, more like 80’s/90s but the best when it comes to covering ILP. Blows AQA chapter 3 out of the water. It’s like if Chapter 3 was a whole book.

Classes:
Coursera Princeton Comparch

Udacity, HPCA from GATECH

You can view either or, but somethings the GATECH course does better, others the Princeton course does better. Both cover the same material, YMMV. Watch both if you can, but skip when necessary.

Phase 3 onwards is reserved for finishing everything above, no small feat I assure you.

Interesting Culture of CA

Better learn about the following things to be in the know:
Why the pentium 4 sucked, John Hennessy, David Patterson, Onur Mutlu, Sohi, Yale N. Patt, University of Texas Austin, University of Wiscon, Madison, NCSU, UC Berkeley, Stanford, Mark Hill, Flynn and his taxonomy, University of Washington and SMT, Robert Tomasulo, MEMORIZE AMDAHLS LAW, Alpha 21264, Intel 8080, AMD 64 X2, AMD64 ISA extension

that’s enough to start into the culture for now. those are like buzz words, they have to mean something to you and get you pumped. If they’re passed in conversation, you should know the significance.
